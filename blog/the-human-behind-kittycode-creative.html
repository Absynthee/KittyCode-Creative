The Human Behind KittyCode Creative (Not a Cat?)

14 August 2024

business
web design agency

KittyCode Creative isn't run by cats alone. Meet Austin Spillman, the human and sole developer behind the new web design agency KittyCode Creative.

Hello! I'm Austin—definitely not a cat, but a real human! I'm an experienced digital designer and front-end web developer with nearly 10 years in the tech and design industries. As the owner and sole developer here at KittyCode Creative, I’m dedicated to delivering high-quality, personalised web solutions. If you’re interested in connecting with me directly you can find me on LinkedIn.

Until recently, I was part of the team at House337, where I designed and developed web pages for Apple's EU and CEMEA sites. This experience greatly boosted my confidence and honed my ability to create high-standard websites. Even before this role, I had a lifelong passion for design. I studied digital design and web development at Anglia Ruskin University, where I also explored 3D modelling, 2D & 3D video production, and audio design. My first role after university was as a volunteer web designer for Young People Littleport (YPL).

I started KittyCode Creative to offer affordable, high-quality web services to small businesses and individuals who want to enhance their online presence without breaking the bank or navigating the confusing "do it yourself" web builder landscape. Thanks to my experience and low overheads as a sole developer, I can provide unique, personalised services at competitive prices.

My approach is straightforward—keeping web design and development simple. This field can be complicated and overwhelming, even for seasoned professionals. With so many platforms, languages, libraries, and third-party services available, it’s easy to get lost. My goal is to cut through the noise and deliver clean, hand-crafted HTML, CSS, and JavaScript as the foundation of my websites. In cases where clients need a robust blog or ready-built Content Management System (CMS), I use Webflow to ensure the best possible experience.

I’m passionate about making the web a better, more inclusive place. My commitment is to create websites that are accessible to everyone, regardless of conditions, disabilities, or devices. This reflects the way I live my life—ensuring that everyone feels included and has a sense of belonging. Accessibility is a core part of KittyCode Creative’s mission.

Making the web accessible benefits individuals, businesses, and society. Your website will be fully perceivable, operable, understandable and robust.

Modern web standards have evolved significantly in recent years—technologies have improved, languages have become more user-friendly, and web builders have gained popularity. Staying up to date with these trends is crucial for providing clients with the best possible experience. This commitment to continuous improvement aligns with KittyCode Creative’s vision for the future. I’ll be writing a post next week about modern CSS, so stay tuned!

You might be wondering—why KittyCode? I chose the name KittyCode Creative partly because of my love for cats, but also because it’s playful and approachable. I wanted to stand out from the more corporate, professional names some businesses use. The word "Creative" highlights that we don’t just focus on development—design is a major part of what we do. I wanted to blend "Code" (which implies hand-crafted coding) and "Creative" together. The idea of cats tapping away at a keyboard is something I love, and thus, KittyCode Creative and its mascot, Code, were born.

If you have any questions or would like to get in touch, you can reach me at hello@kittycodecreative.com or click the button below to visit our contact page. Thank you for reading, and I hope you have a wonderful day!

Austin Spillman - KittyCode Creative
